# Heart Failure Escalation Rules and Clinical Decision Pathways
# Source: ACC/AHA 2022 Guidelines, ESC 2021 Guidelines, Hospital Triage Protocols
# For demonstration purposes only — not for clinical use

## IMMEDIATE ICU ESCALATION — Act within 1 hour

Escalate to ICU immediately if ANY of the following:
- Ejection fraction below 20% with signs of acute decompensation
- Systolic blood pressure below 80 mmHg not responding to fluids
- Creatinine rising more than 0.5 mg/dL in 24 hours
- Serum sodium below 120 mEq/L
- Oxygen saturation below 88% on room air
- Signs of cardiogenic shock: cold extremities, altered consciousness,
  urine output below 30mL/hour
- Ventricular tachycardia or fibrillation on ECG
- New onset complete heart block

Action: Call senior cardiologist IMMEDIATELY
        Activate ICU bed
        Prepare IV access, continuous monitoring
        Consider IV inotropes (Dobutamine starting dose 2.5 mcg/kg/min)

## URGENT CARDIOLOGY REVIEW — Act within 4 hours

Refer urgently to cardiology if:
- Ejection fraction below 30% (new finding or acute decline)
- Kidney-heart risk pattern: creatinine above 1.5 AND EF below 30%
- Serum sodium 120-130 mEq/L with worsening symptoms
- Creatinine above 2.0 mg/dL in setting of heart failure
- New significant mitral or aortic valve dysfunction
- Patient with EF below 35% not yet referred for ICD evaluation
- Rapid deterioration from previously stable state

Action: Contact on-call cardiologist
        Obtain urgent ECG and chest X-ray
        Review and adjust current medications
        Consider hospital admission

## SAME DAY CLINICAL REVIEW — Act within 12 hours

Schedule same day review for:
- Ejection fraction 30-40% with new symptoms
- Creatinine 1.5-2.0 mg/dL (rising trend)
- Sodium 130-135 mEq/L (hyponatremia)
- Weight gain 2kg in 2 days or 3kg in 1 week
- Increasing breathlessness, leg swelling, or fatigue
- Comorbidity score of 2 with worsening clinical picture

Action: Arrange same day appointment or review
        Check electrolytes and renal function urgently
        Review diuretic dosing
        Patient education on fluid and salt restriction

## ROUTINE FOLLOW UP — Standard protocol

Routine monitoring appropriate for:
- Ejection fraction above 40%
- Creatinine below 1.2 mg/dL (stable)
- Sodium above 135 mEq/L (normal)
- No acute deterioration
- Well-compensated on current medications
- Comorbidity score 0-1 with stable markers

Action: Schedule follow up in 4-12 weeks
        Repeat bloods at next visit
        Continue current medications
        Reinforce lifestyle advice

## SPECIFIC CLINICAL SCENARIOS

SCENARIO: Patient with EF 20%, creatinine 1.9, sodium 128
Risk level: CRITICAL
Action: IMMEDIATE ICU escalation
        Call senior cardiologist NOW
        Do not delay for further tests
        IV access, continuous monitoring, prepare inotrope support

SCENARIO: Patient with EF 28%, creatinine 1.6, sodium 133
Risk level: HIGH
Action: URGENT cardiology review within 4 hours
        Consider hospital admission
        Review ACE inhibitor dosing (may need dose reduction)
        Cardiology input on ICD referral

SCENARIO: Patient with EF 35%, creatinine 1.4, sodium 137
Risk level: MEDIUM
Action: Same day review
        Optimize GDMT
        Check if on all four medication pillars
        ICD evaluation if EF remains below 35%

SCENARIO: Patient with EF 45%, creatinine 1.0, sodium 140
Risk level: LOW
Action: Routine follow up in 6-12 weeks
        Continue current management
        Reinforce lifestyle modification
        Annual echocardiogram

## DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

Every clinical interaction must record:
1. Timestamp of assessment
2. Ejection fraction value and source (echo date)
3. Creatinine and sodium at time of assessment
4. Risk level assigned (Low/Medium/High/Critical)
5. Clinical action taken
6. Clinician responsible
7. Next review date

This audit trail is required for clinical governance and 
medico-legal protection. All predictions from this AI system
are logged automatically to the predictions database with
full timestamp and patient identifier.
