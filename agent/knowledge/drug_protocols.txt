# Heart Failure Drug Protocols
# Source: ACC/AHA 2022 Guidelines, ESC 2021 Guidelines
# For demonstration purposes only — not for clinical use
# Always verify with current prescribing guidelines before clinical use

## FIRST LINE THERAPY FOR HFrEF (EF BELOW 40%)

The four pillars of guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT):

1. ACE INHIBITOR or ARB or ARNI
   Examples: Enalapril, Lisinopril (ACE); Valsartan (ARB); 
             Sacubitril/Valsartan — Entresto (ARNI — preferred)
   Starting dose: Enalapril 2.5mg twice daily
   Target dose: Enalapril 10-20mg twice daily
   Purpose: Reduces afterload, prevents cardiac remodeling
   Contraindications: Bilateral renal artery stenosis, pregnancy,
                      history of angioedema, creatinine >3.0 mg/dL

2. BETA-BLOCKER
   Examples: Carvedilol, Metoprolol succinate, Bisoprolol
   Starting dose: Carvedilol 3.125mg twice daily
   Target dose: Carvedilol 25mg twice daily
   Purpose: Reduces heart rate, prevents sudden cardiac death
   Contraindications: Acute decompensated HF, severe bradycardia,
                      severe asthma, cardiogenic shock
   Note: Must be euvolemic before starting — do not start in fluid overload

3. MINERALOCORTICOID RECEPTOR ANTAGONIST (MRA)
   Examples: Spironolactone, Eplerenone
   Starting dose: Spironolactone 25mg daily
   Target dose: Spironolactone 25-50mg daily
   Purpose: Reduces fibrosis, improves survival
   Contraindications: Creatinine >2.5 mg/dL (male) or >2.0 (female),
                      potassium above 5.0 mEq/L, significant renal impairment

4. SGLT2 INHIBITOR
   Examples: Dapagliflozin (Farxiga), Empagliflozin (Jardiance)
   Dose: Dapagliflozin 10mg daily
   Purpose: Reduces hospitalization and cardiovascular death
   Recommended regardless of diabetes status
   Contraindications: eGFR below 25, recurrent UTIs

## DIURETICS FOR FLUID OVERLOAD

LOOP DIURETICS (first choice for volume overload):
   Examples: Furosemide (Lasix), Bumetanide, Torsemide
   Starting dose: Furosemide 20-40mg daily
   Adjust based on fluid status and kidney function
   Monitor: Creatinine, electrolytes, daily weight
   Goal: Remove 0.5-1.0 kg per day in chronic overload
         Remove 1.0-2.0 kg per day in acute decompensation

THIAZIDE DIURETICS (add-on for diuretic resistance):
   Example: Metolazone 2.5-5mg 30 minutes before furosemide
   Use cautiously — monitor electrolytes closely

## SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS BY RISK LEVEL

LOW RISK PATIENTS (EF 40-50%, normal creatinine, normal sodium):
- Optimize GDMT to target doses
- Lifestyle modification: salt restriction, fluid restriction
- Regular monitoring: every 3-6 months
- Exercise cardiac rehabilitation recommended

MEDIUM RISK PATIENTS (EF 30-40%, creatinine 1.2-1.5):
- Start or optimize all four GDMT pillars
- Increase monitoring frequency: every 4-8 weeks
- Consider device therapy evaluation (ICD if EF <35%)
- Nephrology input if creatinine trending upward

HIGH RISK PATIENTS (EF below 30%, creatinine above 1.5):
- Immediate specialist review
- Caution with ACE inhibitors — may worsen kidney function
- Reduce or hold diuretics if creatinine rising acutely
- Consider inpatient admission for optimization
- ICD referral strongly recommended if EF <35%
- Cardiac transplant evaluation if refractory

CRITICAL PATIENTS (EF below 20%, multi-organ involvement):
- ICU admission
- IV inotropes may be required (Dobutamine, Milrinone)
- Continuous hemodynamic monitoring
- Urgent cardiology and nephrology co-management
- Mechanical circulatory support consideration (IABP, LVAD)
- Palliative care discussion if appropriate

## MONITORING PARAMETERS

Renal function monitoring:
- Check creatinine and electrolytes within 1-2 weeks of any dose change
- If creatinine rises more than 30% above baseline — hold ACE/ARB
- If potassium rises above 5.5 mEq/L — hold MRA immediately

Blood pressure targets:
- Target systolic BP: 100-130 mmHg in HFrEF
- Do not titrate GDMT if systolic BP below 90 mmHg
- Hypotension with symptoms — reduce diuretics first

Weight monitoring:
- Daily weight at same time each morning
- Weight gain of 2kg in 2 days or 3kg in 1 week:
  Increase diuretic dose or contact clinical team immediately
